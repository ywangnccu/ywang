Women’s History Month is dedicated to recognize and honor the achievements, contributions, and impact of brave talented women in historical events and contemporary society. Presidents have issued annual proclamations to commemorate the month. 
Each year, the National Women’s History Alliance selects a theme for educational programs and public events. The theme of this year is Leading the Change - Women Shaping a Sustainable Future. This theme affirms fostering systems that support people and the earth. 
Women’s History Month highlights women’s accomplishments, ensures their legacies are remembered and their impact acknowledged, encourages learning about role models from diverse backgrounds, 
and inspires equality, respect, and opportunity for everyone. People celebrate Women’s History Month by reading biographies and documentaries about influential women, participating in school or library events, 
creating a poster or timeline of women in history, sharing accomplishments of women who made a difference in our community, shaped our past and are actively building our future. Women should be celebrated every month. 
However, a month dedicated to female empowerment is extremely special.
